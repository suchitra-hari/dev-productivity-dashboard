## Key Insights

### Speed

- **Delivery Loop** shipped the most PRs (178) but velocity dropped sharply from W2 (12.4/person) to W4 (4.1/person) — consistent with a large initiative peak (likely the PNP 4.2 migration) tapering off.
- **Design System** and **Build Loop** show a strong and consistent upward velocity trend across all 4 weeks — likely driven by agentic tooling adoption ramping up.
- **Agent Loop (Developer Platform)** maintained steady output (~3.7 PRs/person/week) across the period.
- **Shared Services** is at low volume (17 PRs, 5 members) — worth checking if this reflects planned focus or capacity constraints.

### Effectiveness (DXI — Jan snapshot)

- **Build Loop** is significantly below org average (DXI 45, −14 vs org). This warrants a follow-up survey. Build loop tooling improvements (NX, Webpack, TypeScript) may not yet be reflected.
- **Shared Services** leads at DXI 74 (+15 vs org) — team health signal is strong.
- Next snapshot will capture impact of Cursor adoption ramp.

### Quality (Defect Ratio — live Jira data)

- All teams are very low on bug fixes (<4% of closed issues are bugs), which is healthy.
- **Delivery Loop** (3.2%) and **Agent Loop** (2.9%) have the highest defect ratios — worth watching as velocity stays high.

### Impact (Innovation Ratio — live Jira data)

- **Delivery Loop** is outstanding at 59.2% of closed issues being features/stories — clearly in execution mode.
- **Agent Loop** at 21.3% — a mix of platform/infrastructure work and feature delivery.
- **Design System** and **Build Loop** are low (<4%) on innovation ratio, reflecting that most of their Jira work is tasks/tech debt (DDA migration, infrastructure work).

### Agentic Adoption

- **4 of 5 teams** qualify as Full-Agentic (>50% of members used Cursor Agent in the 30d period).
- **Delivery Loop** leads in raw agent volume: 2,442 total agent requests. Dustin Neighly accounts for the majority — ~1,000+ requests in W3 alone with extremely high acceptance rates.
- **Design System** is at 100% team adoption but smaller volume; W3 saw 779 total applies (highest code application week).
- **Build Loop** is the lagging team at 25% Cursor adoption — only 2 of 8 members using agent mode. This correlates with their lower DXI.
- **Agent Loop** members are highly agentic (64% adoption) and generate substantial output.

### Re-review Rate

- **Agent Loop** (avg 12.7%) and **Delivery Loop** (avg 10.0%) have the highest re-review rates. This is expected for teams shipping high volumes of complex work — more changes means more feedback cycles.
- **Design System** (1.6%) and **Build Loop** (3.5%) are very clean — either smaller PRs or high trust between reviewers.
- Re-review rate does **not** negatively correlate with agentic adoption in this data — high-agentic teams aren't seeing worse review quality.

## Methodology & Notes

| Signal                    | Source                                        | Definition                                                               |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Speed                     | DX warehouse `github_pulls`                   | PRs merged per week, PRs/person/week                                     |
| Effectiveness (DXI)       | DX survey snapshots `dx_snapshot_team_scores` | Composite developer experience score (survey-based, 0–100)               |
| Quality (Defect Ratio)    | DX warehouse `jira_issues`                    | Bug/Defect issues closed ÷ total issues closed                           |
| Impact (Innovation Ratio) | DX warehouse `jira_issues`                    | Story/Feature issues closed ÷ total issues closed                        |
| Agentic Adoption          | DX warehouse `cursor_daily_user_metrics`      | % of team members with active Cursor Agent sessions (agent_requests > 0) |
| Agent Requests            | DX warehouse `cursor_daily_user_metrics`      | Total Cursor Agent requests per team per week                            |
| Re-review Rate            | DX warehouse `github_reviews`                 | % of merged PRs with CHANGES_REQUESTED followed by subsequent review     |
| CI Failure Rate           | Buildkite MCP                                 | Failed builds ÷ total builds (pipeline → team heuristic classification)  |

**Team → DX mapping:**

- Design System = Spring Design System (team_id 58)
- Build Loop = Build Loop (team_id 50)
- Agent Loop = Developer Platform (team_id 21)
- Delivery Loop = Delivery Loop (team_id 23)
- Shared Services = Shared Services (team_id 3)

**DXI note:** Survey snapshots collected every ~6 weeks. Jan 11–21 snapshot is the most recent. Next snapshot expected ~Mar 2026.

**CI note:** Buildkite failure rates require BUILDKITE_API_TOKEN and run separately. This report used the DX warehouse for all other signals.
